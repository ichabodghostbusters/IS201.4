Why Slalom?

Slalom is thrilling and challenging while providing an excellent workout as well.

It is a great summer activity especially on a calm yet warm Spring or Summer day.

Slalom takes more skill and practice than other watersports so there is always somehting to be improving on.


Background

Slalom skiing was invented in 1922 by Ralph Samuelson, although it was a shadow of what the sport is today.

The first run was on Lake Pepin in Lake City, Minnesota. He was still using two skis however at this point in time.

It wasn't until the 1950's that one water ski "slalom" began to surface.

Originally built of wood and quite flat, modern day slalom skis have much more curvature and are made of composites like fiberglass and carbon fiber.
